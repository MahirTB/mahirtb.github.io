Sales Insight of a Global Superstore

Worked on a random store dataset. It had a vast sales data and I checked for inconsistency using filters on Excel, dealt with some formatting issues. After the cleaning part I jumped into the visualization part.

This report presents a comprehensive analysis of the global superstore's performance through a dynamic and insightful Power BI dashboard. The dashboard focuses on three key performance indicators (KPIs) - Total Sales, Total Profit, and Average Shipping Time. The first two KPIs were pretty straight forward, but for the third one I had to use Quick Measure on Power BI. Additionally, it provides users with the flexibility to explore data using three slicers for country, category, and year (2012-2015).

Key Findings:

  1. Total Sales & Profits: The line-column chart displays the monthly sales and profits year by year while the bar chart shows top 5 countries where total sales were the highest.
  2. Customer Segmentation: The pie chart depicts the revenue percentage generated by customer segments (consumer, home office and conporate).
This visualization provides all the key insights. From the line-column chart it is clear that every year Nov-Dec sales are higher. So stragies can be made to invest more on these two months. The pie chart denotes that the most lucrative segment is Consumers. So marketing should team should focus more on consumers.
